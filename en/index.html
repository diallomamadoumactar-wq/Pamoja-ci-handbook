What PAMOJA CI is about

A sexual and reproductive health
narrative infrastructure.

PAMOJA CI is a multi-year communication and Social Behavior Change initiative led by RAES in partnership with Amplify Change. It focuses on unwanted pregnancies, access to reliable SRH information, and the operationalization of Article 427 of the Ivorian Penal Code (June 2024).

The problem in one sentence

The silence around unwanted pregnancies in Côte d'Ivoire is not protection. It is abandonment.

Each year, between 209,000 and 288,000 abortions are performed in Côte d'Ivoire. 63% are unsafe. Article 427 of the Penal Code, amended in June 2024, now authorizes safe abortion in two cases — danger to the mother's life and pregnancy resulting from rape. This law is widely unknown, including among healthcare professionals.

These figures are not a statistical abstraction. They are the result of a social silence that prevents women from seeking the information that would protect them, and of an active underground market (6 Facebook pages identified, 29,000+ cumulative followers) that captures this demand without protecting it.

Voices from the field
You can't go talk to your parents. Not even your mother. You call your friend, she knows someone who knows someone, and that's how it goes. — Focus group, women aged 20-30, Abidjan · RAES Formative Research 2024

This quote is not an anecdote. It signals half of the problem PAMOJA CI addresses: the guidance journey of young women facing unwanted pregnancies goes through informal, untrained and often poorly-equipped channels. The other half: an underground market that has understood this dynamic and established itself there.
