The model

NED — Narrative
Ecosystem Design.

NED is a discipline of narrative infrastructure architecture. The PAMOJA project in Côte d'Ivoire is one application — not a unique instantiation. This page describes the model itself, its roles, its principles, and how it is measured.

01 Definition 02 Architecture 03 Roles 04 Governance 05 Principles 06 Measurement 07 Distinction 08 Life cycle
01Definition

What NED is — and what it is not.

Narrative Ecosystem Design is a discipline of narrative infrastructure architecture. It aims to emerge, stabilize, and disseminate dominant narratives favorable to social or behavioral change, through the orchestration of an interconnected ecosystem of actors, stories, platforms, and social dynamics.

It borrows from several traditions without being reducible to any of them: narrative reframing from FrameWorks Institute, cultural convergence documented by the Norman Lear Center, classical Social Behavior Change on behaviors and norms, systems thinking applied to narratives.

NED IS

  • A discipline in development
  • A grammar of ecosystem architecture
  • A set of principles and methods
  • Transposable to different contexts and subjects

NED is NOT

  • A creative briefing methodology
  • A 360 campaign strategy
  • A stabilized academic theory
  • A final deliverable or a project brand

03Roles

Four roles in a NED ecosystem.

A living NED is organized around four interdependent roles. Clarity of these roles is the condition of non-redundancy — and of non-dependence on a central structure that would try to control everything.

Role · 01

The architect

In PAMOJA CI: RAES

Designs the ecosystem, defines the strategic framework, identifies actors, validates the moral coherence of the whole. Does not produce content — orchestrates those who do.

Role · 02

The laboratory

In PAMOJA CI: OASIS

Research, testing, measurement, refinement of methodology. Surfaces weak signals, documents learnings, adjusts the model across cycles. Safeguard against the framework becoming rigid.

Role · 03

The producers

In PAMOJA CI: 6 categories of actors

Create content, carry the stories in their media. Autonomous in their creativity, aligned on the shared moral compass. Digital creators, radio teams, production houses, CSOs, religious leaders, journalists.

Role · 04

The recipients

In PAMOJA CI: 6 personas

Not passive. As norms shift, some recipients become relays (Tantie Affoué, Pastor Mariam). The ecosystem strengthens itself through this conversion — a condition for its resilience without central control.

04Governance

Three governance levels not to confuse.

The confusion between "NED" and "PAMOJA CI" is frequent — and blurs the conversation with funders, partners, and teams. Three distinct levels coexist:

  • Discipline — NED. Methodological corpus in development, carried by RAES / OASIS. Transposable to other contexts, other subjects, other countries. Not proprietary to the PAMOJA project.
  • Application — The PAMOJA project in Côte d'Ivoire. The framework, the matrix, the briefs, the services, the deliverables. Application of NED to the Ivorian SRH context. This is the scope of this handbook.
  • Cycles — The annual campaigns. 2026, 2027, 2028. Each cycle produces learning and adjustment — of the local framework (application) and of the model (discipline).
05Principles

Five methodological principles.

What distinguishes a well-designed narrative ecosystem from a standard campaign — and what guides decisions under ambiguity.

i.

Convergence before control

We don't control each piece of content. We ensure the ecosystem produces morally convergent content. Coherence comes through the shared moral compass, not through a validation committee.

ii.

Density before virality

An effective narrative cloud is permanent and dense. Occasional virality doesn't shift norms. Twenty aligned, average contents shift more than one isolated viral piece.

iii.

Landing before diffusion

Creating demand without real landing points amplifies underground circuits. The services ecosystem must be mapped as thoroughly as the narrative ecosystem.

iv.

Moral compass before editorial line

Coherence lies in the underlying values, not in the style guide. A pastor, a TikToker, and a journalist can say the same thing in different words if the compass is shared.

v.

Long cycle before single shot

A NED is measured in years, not seasons. Campaigns are moments of intensification — the infrastructure continues between cycles.

06Measurement

How a narrative ecosystem is measured.

Tactical indicators (views, reach, engagement) remain useful for steering individual tactics. But they do not measure NED. Four strategic indicators do.

Narrative density

Volume of morally convergent contents circulating autonomously, including those RAES has not produced or directly validated. Indicator of ecosystem health.

Norms shift

Measured by repeated social listening and qualitative research at regular intervals. Social norms shift slowly — tracking must be patient and long-term.

Landing absorption

Ratio between demand generated by content and demand effectively absorbed by safe formal services. A low ratio signals a dangerous campaign.

Infrastructure resilience

Ability of the system to continue producing convergent narratives without central intervention. Measured by the persistence of outputs between intensification cycles.

07Distinction

What distinguishes NED from comparable approaches.

NED borrows from several traditions — but is not reducible to any of them. Here are the structuring differences with approaches often confused with it.

Approach Main focus Difference from NED
Classical SBC Behaviors and norms in a defined audience NED works on narrative infrastructure upstream of behaviors. SBC remains a component of NED, not a substitute.
360 Communication Multichannel coherence of a unified message NED orchestrates stories carried by diverse actors with their own voice — not a unified message. Diversity of voices is a strength.
Advocacy Moving decision-makers on a specific policy NED works on the cultural environment that makes policy acceptance possible — it precedes and feeds advocacy.
Brand campaign Identity and awareness of a brand NED doesn't carry a brand, it carries shared stories. A PAMOJA actor can be invisible to the final audience.
08Life cycle

The life cycle of a NED application.

An applied NED does not unfold in a single campaign. It follows a long cycle combining intensification moments (annual campaigns) and periods where the infrastructure continues between cycles.

Before · 6-12 months

Diagnostic

Narrative mapping, actor mapping, strategic framework, tactical matrix, landing services cartography. This is where the PAMOJA project in CI stands today.

During · 3-6 months

Intensification

Coordinated activation of producers, continuous signal monitoring, tactical adjustments. Contents circulate, norms start shifting.

After · 6-12 months

Capitalization

Evaluation, field feedback, framework and model adjustment. Preparation of the next cycle with integrated learnings.

Long term · 3-5 years

Infrastructure

The ecosystem continues producing convergent narratives between cycles. Trained aunties remain trained. Progressive leaders continue preaching. Media treat the subject.
